International English Language Testing System

IELTS (International English Language Testing System) is the world’s most popular English language test. More than 3 million IELTS tests are taken each year. Taking IELTS opens doors – it can help you live, study and work around the world. More than 10,000 organisations in 140 countries accept IELTS, including schools, universities, employers, immigration authorities and professional bodies. IELTS assesses all of your English skills — reading, writing, listening and speaking, and is designed to reflect how you will use English at study, at work, and at play, in your new life abroad.

Exam Pattern

  • Listening : It has 4 sections and 40 questions in all. It lasts for 40 minutes including the time one gets for transferring the answers to the main answer sheet.
  • Reading : Reading has 3 passages, 40 questions in all. Candidates are given only 1 hour to complete reading, which means that time management is very important. One must give 20 minutes per passage.
  • Writing :It has 2 writing tasks. Task 1 is primarily Report writing. Students are advised to spend 20 minutes on this task. Task 2 is Argumentative essay. Students are advised to spend 40 minutes on the same. In all, time allotted for writing is 1 hour.
  • Speaking : Speaking has 3 parts and lasts for 11-14 minutes. It is done on one-to-one basis with the student. Part 1, Examiner asks general questions to the student, which relates to his/her work, studies, hobbies etc. Part 2, The candidate is given a topic to speak. Students have to speak for 1 minute at least and are given one minute to think and write down some points. Part 3, It's a two way discussion with the examiner.
